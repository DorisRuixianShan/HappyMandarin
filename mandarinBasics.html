  • 声调 shēng mǔ (Tones)
    Script of tones Name of tones Description Concept & example Vocal cord
    ā 1st High and level Like singing out an English syllable, 衣yī, sing out i: Keep tightest, almost no change, and the sound is long
    á 2nd Rising (middle low to high) Like doubting: Did you say 王wáng? Not tight not loose, stretch till the tightest
    ǎ 3rd Low (with rise): If read separately, fall then rise; If in the context with other characters, aiming at low (except in two consecutive third tones) Aiming at low when read together with other characters in contexts; 很高hěn gāo,书法 shū fǎ Loosen and extend, suddenly tighten, but not to the tightest; if read separately, the longest, but in the context with other characters, short
    à 4th Falling (very high then low) Like LIST final intonation or Certainty. Like counting, 1, 2, 3 in all rising tone and then the last one 4 in falling tone Tight–loosen till completely loose, and the sound is short
    a Light tone Toneless, depending on contexts
